Intro

The Square Kilometer Array (SKA), which will be the largest radio telescope in the world, has vastly increased the focus on astronomical research in our country. The size and complexity of this telescope(not to mention the future SKA) will vastly increase radio astronomical data output, generating multi-terabyte data cubes that are several orders of magnitude larger than the current norm.There is currently a UCT focus on visualization for Big Data in Astronomy. We have a collaboration with the Astronomy Department at UCT to build software tools to assist with processing and analysing the increased volume of data. A previous (published*) Honours project designed the AstroVis system to enable simple 3D visualisation of very large astronomical data cubes on desktop computers. In this project, you will extend this project, and develop a new visualizations or combined existing approaches innovatively to enable astronomers to explore and compare 3D radio astronomy data cubes.
